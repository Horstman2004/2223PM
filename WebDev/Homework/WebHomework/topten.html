1 Ball State Has a meteorology degree. Always been interested in atmospheric science so this would be one of my top picks. It’s also recognized by the NWS for reaching all their standards. Closer to an actual NWS office. Allows for more interaction in the field that I want to go into. Host field meteorology. Difference between broadcast and field is that you're actually out analyzing data in a field, broadcast you are on a tv in everyone’s household. Has options for Computer Science as well. Fact: Computer Science is my first option.
2 Valparaiso Similar to Ball State as far as accreditation goes. Has a very good program for broadcast meteorology. Bit farther away than I’d like to be but still a viable option in my book.
3 Western Kentucky University Same as ball state, bigger campus, more of a party school. Not my top pick for what I want to do but overall still a great college for atmospheric sciences. In state tuition as well. Has a great field meteorology program. School is a lot closer to the NWS office that hosts the quad states region including us. Computer Science classes are small which is good. A big class isn’t always the best.
4 Ivy Tech Plan to get my general classes needed for what I want to major in. More cheaper option then going to a 4 year school.
5 USI Doesn’t have a meteorology degree sadly but does have an excellent program for Computer Science, Web Development, and Game Development. It is very close to home. I wouldn't have to pay for residency at the University.
6 Vincennes University University is fairly close to where I live. Has quite a vast variety of engineering degrees. Great school considering distance, degrees, and accreditation. I would have to pay for residency. It's is a little less than an hour drive to get there.
7 Murray Has a great Cyber Security program. I'd enjoy going through the programs and work in a Cyber Security field, but it's not my first preference.
8 Oklahoma University Choose it for meterology. Has the best meterology program in the US. I would totally go here if I didn't feel like moving across the country.
